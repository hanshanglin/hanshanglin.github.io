Statistical Inference - Probability

Probability space ($C$, $B$, $P$)

$C$: Sample sapce or set of all possible outcomes.
$B$: Collection of subsets of $C$.
$P$: $P(A)$ probability of event $A$.

  1. $P(A)\geq0, \forall A \in B$
  2. $P(C)=1$
  3. $P(\cup^\infty_{i=1}A_i) = \sum^\infty_{i=1}P(A_i)$ (disjoint case)

Random variable

$X:C \to R, R$: set of real numbers.
$\forall x \in R, {X \leq x}$ is an event.
$F(x) = P(X\leq x)$ is cdf of $X$.

Type of rvs

Discrete rvs

pmf: probability mass function

$$ p_X(x) = P(X=x), x \in D $$
$$ 0 \leq p_X(x) \leq 1, and \sum _{x\in D}p_X(x)=1 $$

Continuous rvs

pdf: probability density function

$$ f(x)=F'(x) \\ f(x) \geq 0 , \int^\infty_\infty f(x)dx = 1 \\ F(x) = \int^x_{-\infty} f(y)dy $$

Transformations :

$Y=g(x)$ , and $g$ is one-to-one, $x = g^{-1}(y)$

$$f_Y(y) = f_X(g^{-1}(y)) \left| \frac{dx}{dy} \right| $$

eg. $f$ the pdf of $X$ is $f(x) = 2xe^{‚àíx^2}$, $0 < x < \infty$, zero elsewhere, determine the pdf of $Y = X^2$

$$ \begin{aligned} Y &= g(x) = x^2 \\ f_Y(y) &= f_X(\sqrt{y})\left| 2x \right| \\ &= 2\sqrt{y}e^{‚àíy} \frac{1}{2\sqrt y} \\ &=e^{-y} \\ f_Y(y)&= \left\{ \begin{array}{lr} e^{-y} , & 0 < y < \infty\\ 0 , &else \end{array} \right. \end{aligned} $$

Distributional quantities

Mean or expectation of $X$

$$ \begin{aligned} E(X) = \left\{ \begin{array}{lr} \sum_xxp(x) ,& if \sum \left| x \right| p(x) < \infty &discret\\ \int^\infty_{-\infty}xf(x)dx,& if \int \left| x \right|f(x)dx<\infty & continuous \end{array} \right. \end{aligned} $$

m-th moments

$$ \begin{aligned} E(X^m) = \left\{ \begin{array}{lr} \sum_xx^mp(x) ,& if \sum \left| x \right|^m p(x) < \infty &discret\\ \int^\infty_{-\infty}x^mf(x)dx,& if \int \left| x \right|^mf(x)dx<\infty & continuous \end{array} \right. \end{aligned} $$

Variance

$$ \sigma^2 = Var(x)=E(X-E(X))^2=EX^2-(E(X))^2 $$

Moment generating function (mgf)

if $E(e^{tX})$ exists for $\left| t \right| < h$,the mgf of $X$ is defined to be the function $M(t) = E(e^{tX},\left| t \right| <h )$.
property: $M^{(m)}(0) = E(X^m)$ (can be used for mean and variance)

Multivariate distributions

Random Vector

$$X=(X_1,X_2,...,X_m)^T$$

Distributions

Joint cdf

$$F_{X_1,X_2}(x_1,x_2) = P(X_1\leq x_1,X_2\leq x_2)$$

Discrete case (pmf)

$$ p_{X_1,X_2}(x_1,x_2) = P(X_1 = x_1,X_2 = x_2) $$

Continuous case (pdf)

$$f_{X_1,X_2}(x_1,x_2) = \frac{\partial^2F_{X_1,X_2}(x_1,x_2)}{\partial{x_1}{x_2} }$$
$$F_{X_1,X_2}(x_1,x_2) = \int^{x_1}_{-\infty}{\int^{x_2}_{-\infty}{f_{X_1,X_2}(y_1,y_2)dy_1}dy_2} $$

Marginals

$$F_{X_1}(x_1) = F_{X_1,X_2}(x_1,\infty)$$
$$ p_{X_1}(x_1) = \sum_{x_2}p_{X_1,X_2}(x_1,x_2) $$
$$F_{X_1}(x_1) = {\int^{\infty }_{-\infty}{f_{X_1,X_2}(x_1,x_2)}dx_2} $$

Jacobian of the transformation

$$ J = \left| \begin{matrix} {\frac{\partial x_1}{\partial y_1}}&{\frac{\partial x_1}{\partial y_2}} \\ {\frac{\partial x_2}{\partial y_1}}&{\frac{\partial x_2}{\partial y_2}} \end{matrix} \right| $$
$$ F_{Y_1,Y_2}(y_1,y_2) = f_{X_1,X_2}(w_1(y_1,y_2),w_2(y_1,y_2))\left| J \right| $$

Expectation

Let $Y = g(X_1,X_2)$

$$ \begin{aligned} E(Y)=\left\{ \begin{array}{lr} \sum_{x_1}\sum_{x_2}g(x_1,x_2)p(x_1,x_2), &discrete \\ \int^{\infty}_{-\infty}{\int^{\infty}_{-\infty}{g(x_1,x_2)f(x_1,x_2)}dx_1 }dx_2 , &continuous \end{array} \right. \end{aligned} $$
$$ Cov(X_1,X_2) = E\left[\{X_1-E(X_1)\}\{X_2-E(X_2)\} \right] = E(X_1X_2)-E(X_1)E(X_2) $$

Correlation coefficient:

$$\rho = \frac{Cov(X_1,X_2)}{\sigma_1 \sigma_2} $$

Conditional distributions

pmf: $p_{X_2|X_1}(x_1,x_2|x_1)=\frac{p_{X_1,X_2}(x_1,x_2)}{p_{X_1}(x_1)}$
pdf: $f_{X_2|X_1}(x_1,x_2|x_1)=\frac{f_{X_1,X_2}(x_1,x_2)}{f_{X_1}(x_1)}$

Independence

$X_1$ and $X_2$ are said to be mutually independent

$$ \begin{aligned} \Leftrightarrow\left\{ \begin{array}{lr} p_{X_1,X_2}(x_1,x_2) = p_{X_1}(x_1) p_{X_2}(x_2), &discrete \\ f_{X_1,X_2}(x_1,x_2) = f_{X_1}(x_1) f_{X_2}(x_2), &continuous \end{array} \right. \end{aligned} $$
